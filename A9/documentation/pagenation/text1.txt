&lt;br /&gt;&lt;br /&gt;The result is an intimate, confessional, lo-fi masterpiece.&amp;nbsp; I&amp;nbsp; know I&#39;ve overused the reference to &lt;a href=&quot;http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liz_Phair&quot;&gt;Liz Phair&lt;/a&gt;&#39;s &quot;&lt;a href=&quot;http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Girly_Sound&quot;&gt;Girlysound&lt;/a&gt;&quot; tapes, but in this case the reference is unavoidable (and I&#39;m not the only one, see this &lt;a href=&quot;http://pitchfork.com/features/staff-lists/9018-albums-of-the-year-honorable-mention/2/&quot;&gt;Pitchfork review&lt;/a&gt;).&amp;nbsp; (I suppose I could also compare it to the &quot;&lt;a href=&quot;http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Texas_Campfire_Tapes&quot;&gt;Texas Campfire Tapes&lt;/a&gt;&quot;, but those references have gone out of style now that &lt;a href=&quot;http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michelle_Shocked&quot;&gt;Michelle Shocked&lt;/a&gt; is a &lt;a href=&quot;http://www.queerty.com/alt-folk-singer-michelle-shocked-goes-on-homophobic-rant-tells-audience-god-hates-fags-20130318&quot;&gt;self-hating traitor&lt;/a&gt;.)&amp;nbsp; I know the idea of a solo, acoustic, coffee house, autobiographical, neo-hippie, female folk singer is a 3rd stage &lt;a href=&quot;http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lilith_Fair&quot;&gt;Lilith Fair&lt;/a&gt; cliche, but trust me: Katie&#39;s different.&amp;nbsp; There is a unpretentious, wrenching, earnest, piercing sincerity that transcends the sparse, almost harsh recording.&amp;nbsp; Where Liz channeled a battle-of-the-sexes anger borrowed from early &lt;a href=&quot;http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pat_Benatar&quot;&gt;Pat Benatar&lt;/a&gt;, the villain in Katie&#39;s songs is Katie: paralyzed with self-doubt and a &lt;a href=&quot;http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millennials&quot;&gt;millennial&lt;/a&gt;-flavored self-absorption.&amp;nbsp; Despite this, and the fact that I&#39;m nearly old enough to be her father, the music connects with me, in part because Katie&#39;s provided a universal soundtrack for awkward, early 20s relationships (e.g., I&#39;d like to imagine a particular college relationship-but-not-quite-girlfriend addressing me in the manner of &quot;&lt;a href=&quot;http://songmeanings.com/songs/view/3530822107859426371/&quot;&gt;Bathtub&lt;/a&gt;&quot;
